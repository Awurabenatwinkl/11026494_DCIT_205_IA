Administrative Staff: Administrative staff members manage the administrative tasks and day-to-day operations of the CS Department. They handle activities such as departmental budgeting, scheduling classes and exams, coordinating meetings and events, managing departmental communications, maintaining records and databases, and providing general administrative support to faculty, students, and visitors. They are often the first point of contact for inquiries and provide assistance in navigating departmental procedures and policies.

Department Chair/Director

The department chair or director is a senior faculty member who oversees the overall management and strategic direction of the CS Department. They are responsible for setting academic goals and objectives, leading curriculum development, managing faculty appointments and promotions, fostering research initiatives, representing the department in university-wide matters, and ensuring the department's compliance with institutional policies and regulations. The department chair or director often serves as a liaison between faculty, staff, and university administration.

Technical Support Staff: Technical support staff members provide technical assistance and support to students, faculty, and staff within the CS Department. They maintain and troubleshoot computer systems, networks, and software applications used in teaching and research. They assist with hardware and software installations, system upgrades, network configurations, and user account management. They also provide guidance on the use of specialized equipment, programming languages, development tools, and other technical resources within the department.

Academic Advisors

Academic advisors in the CS Department assist students in planning and managing their academic journey. They provide guidance on course selection, degree requirements, academic policies, and career pathways. Academic advisors help students create personalized academic plans, monitor their progress, address academic challenges, and explore opportunities such as internships, research projects, and study abroad programs. They play a crucial role in supporting students' academic and personal development, helping them navigate their educational goals and make informed decisions.
